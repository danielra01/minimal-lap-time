In order to formulate the optimization problem, we need to have some controls of the car. These are given as 
\begin{align*}
    u(t) = \begin{bmatrix} a(t) \\ \delta(t) \end{bmatrix}
\end{align*}
with $a$ being the acceleration and $\delta$ being the steering angle. With this, we can finally formulate the optimization problem.
Using the model above, we can formulate the problem as follows
\begin{align}
    \underset{z(\cdot),\,u(\cdot),\,T}{\operatorname{minimize}}
    \quad & T
    \label{eq:objective}
    \\[0.5em]
    \text{subject to}
    \quad
    & \dot{z}(t) = f\big(z(t),u(t)\big),
    & t \in [0,T],
    \label{eq:dynamics}
    \\[0.3em]
    & -\frac{w\big(s(t)\big)-b}{2}
      \leq e_y(t)
      \leq
      \frac{w\big(s(t)\big)-b}{2},
    \label{eq:track_constraint}
    \\[0.3em]
    & -a_{\min} \leq a(t) \leq a_{\max},
    \label{eq:acceleration_constraint}
    \\[0.3em]
    & -\delta_{\max} \leq \delta(t) \leq \delta_{\max},
    \label{eq:steering_constraint}
    \\[0.3em]
    & 0 \leq v(t) \leq v_{\max},
    \label{eq:velocity_constraint}
    \\[0.3em]
    & \left|
        \frac{v(t)^2}{L}\tan\big(\delta(t)\big)
      \right|
      \leq a_{y,\max},
    \label{eq:lateral_acceleration_constraint}
    \\[0.3em]
    & s(0)=0,
    \qquad
      s(T)=S,
    \label{eq:position_boundary_conditions}
    \\[0.3em]
    & v(0)=0,
    \qquad
      e_y(0)=0,
    \qquad
      e_\psi(0)=0.
    \label{eq:initial_conditions}
\end{align}

The constants used in the optimal-control problem are defined as follows:
\begin{description}
    \item[\(w(s)\)] track width at the longitudinal position \(s\),
    \item[\(b\)] width of the vehicle,
    \item[\(L\)] wheelbase of the vehicle,
    \item[\(a_{\min}\)] magnitude of the maximum permissible deceleration,
    \item[\(a_{\max}\)] maximum permissible acceleration,
    \item[\(\delta_{\max}\)] maximum absolute steering angle,
    \item[\(v_{\max}\)] maximum permissible velocity,
    \item[\(a_{y,\max}\)] maximum permissible lateral acceleration,
    \item[\(S\)] total length of the track.
\end{description}
